Aidan Zerdoum

Biomedical Researcher

Education

Ph.D: University of Delaware - June 2019
B.Eng: Stevens Institute of Technology - May 2012

Research Experience

Department of Biomedical Engineering, University of Delaware
Collagen/Hyaluronic Acid Semi-Interpenetrating Matrix for Connective Tissue Engineering
-Optimized protocols for cell encapsulation to promote cell spreading while maintaining viability.
-Coordinated efforts of a team of researchers to assess effects of collagen content on wound healing response at the transcript, protein and cell levels.
-Effectively utilized ELISA, western, and qPCR techniques to analyze cell activity/phenotype.
Investigating the Effects of Vibration on Mesenchymal Stem Cells (hMSCs)
-Modified existing bioreactor design to improve reliability of vibrational stimulation and characterized mechanical properties.
-Supervised a team of 3 researchers while coordinating the implementation of experimental protocols.
-Pioneered 3D platform to study vibration under physiological conditions present in the vocal fold.
-Utilized confocal microscopy, ELISA, and qPCR to define hMSC phenotypic changes.
Inflammatory Effects of Vibration on Hematopoietic Stem Cells (HSCs)
-Developed cell culture protocol for differentiation of macrophages from CD34+ HSCs.
-Identified problems with current bioreactor culture setup and developed methods to avoid these issues.
-Leveraged flow cytometry and confocal microscopy to assess changes in cell phenotype/commitment.
Synthetic Hyaluronic Acid-Based Hydrogel for Connective Tissue Engineering
-Validated and characterized a novel 3D model of fibrogenesis.
-Led development of a facile method for studying cell migration in 3D hydrogels.
-Verified efficacy of therapeutic treatments in ameliorating fibrosis in vitro.
Laboratory for Multiscale Imaging, Stevens Institute of Technology
Morphological Effects of Quaternary Ammonium Compounds on Staphylococcal Biofilms
-Collaborated with the University Medical Center in Groningen (UMCG), Netherlands to study biofilms as part of collaborative research between Dr. Matthew Libera and Profs. Henk Busscher and Henny van der Mei.
-Utilized FIB-SEM interfacial microscopy to resolve bacterial cell wall damage resulting from quaternary ammonium compound treatment.
-Presented work at the eCMXII: Implant Infection conference in Davos, Switzerland.
